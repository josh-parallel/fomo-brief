The Problem 3 risk scenarios

fomo curates wallet collections — "90%+ Winrate," "High-Efficiency," "Safe & Smart," "New Whales" — displayed in the Insights tab. Users browse, tap a profile, hit Copy Trade. Performance labels are computed from on-chain data.

On-chain performance looks clean. Off-chain, the same wallet is being called out as a wash trader on Twitter/X, flagged in a Discord, or mentioned in a regulatory filing. fomo doesn't know. Their users copy the trade. Money is lost.

⚠ 90% of fomo's users came from TikTok and Instagram. They cannot self-verify. When they lose money on a fomo-promoted wallet, the story goes viral before support can respond.
🔁
Wash Trading
A wallet self-trades to inflate its winrate. Crypto Twitter spots it and posts evidence. fomo users are mid-copy-trade.
📋
Regulatory Flag
An SEC or CFTC action names a wallet or associated entity. fomo is still promoting it in Insights with a "Safe & Smart" label.
📉
Coordinated Dump
A top-leaderboard wallet quietly assembles a position, promotes it to followers, then exits. Off-chain coordination is visible before the on-chain move.

What gets monitored
Twitter/X threads calling out wash trading or manipulation on a specific wallet
Reddit, Discord, and crypto forums flagging a promoted trader as a bot or sybil
News mentions linking a wallet address or entity to SEC/CFTC enforcement
Other copy-trade platforms reporting user losses from the same wallet
Coordinated social activity suggesting a pump-and-dump in progress
✦ "This wallet's winrate is faked via self-trades" triggers. A post about a different wallet with a similar address doesn't. The same story across 10 sources fires once.
Why Now
⚡ Perps changed the risk profile
When fomo was spot-only, a bad wallet cost users a meme token. Now it can wipe an account in minutes.

Since June 11, fomo users can copy-trade leveraged perp positions on Hyperliquid. A manipulated wallet at 5× leverage can wipe a follower's account before they see the first alert — the reputational exposure is categorically different.

fomo's entire product bet is that social trust scales. The leaderboard is fomo's credibility surface. A viral TikTok about a fomo-promoted wallet that rugged is not a support ticket problem — it's a Series C story. At $75M raised and 625K users, a single high-profile incident now has enough surface area to matter.

This is a 2-week window. Perps just launched and the team is heads-down on rollout. Setting up monitors now is cheap insurance before the perps user base gets large enough for a bad event to be costly.
